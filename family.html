Madi rudure ra ri i (Immediate Family)👨🏿 👩🏿 👧🏿 👦🏿

'Madi rudure ra ri i' directly translates to 'a person who has married.' This is what forms the basis of the 'immediate' family which includes the wife, husband and child or children.

Extended family

In Ma’di culture, family extends far beyond the nuclear unit commonly seen in the Western world. To understand family we need to understand Suru, kaka, goti and Laki

Suru (Tribe / clan)

Suru in Ma’di ti is used interchangeably to mean both tribe (e.g. Ma’di, Acholi, Lugbara) and clan (e.g. Patibi, Paloi, Ukeyi).

A clan refers to a group of people who descend from the same ancestor. For example, Patibi, meaning “the descendants of Tibi.”

Just as surnames are often passed down through the father in many Western cultures, in Ma’di, a child inherits their clan (suru) from the father. However, each person also has connections to three other clans.

Let's take a look at Anzoa:

  • Her father is from the clan Ukeyi
  • Her mother is from the clan Puceri
  • Her paternal grandmother is from the clan Padiga
  • Her maternal grandmother is from the clan Pageri

Anzoa’s primary clan is Ukeyi, as she inherits her father’s clan. However, she had connections to Puceri, Padiga and pageri.

Goti (Extended Family)

Goti is the term used for extended family on the father’s side; including siblings, cousins, uncles, and aunts who share the same grandparents.

Everyone from the same clan and with shared grandparents is considered part of the same goti.

Lets look at Anzoa who is from the Ukeyi clan

Her relatives who share the same grandparents and are also from the Ukeyi clan are from the same 'goti'

Kaka

Kaka is a broader term used to describe an extended-extended family. This includes people from:

  • your father’s clan
  • your mother’s clan
  • your paternal grandmother’s clan
  • your maternal grandmother’s clan

Kaka can also include people from different clans who grew up in the same area as one of your parents or grandparents.

Difference between Kaka and Suru

The difference between suru (clan) and kaka is:

  • Suru is inherited by birth and cannot be changed
  • your mother’s clan
  • Kaka is based on lived experience and community ties, and can shift depending on where you grow up
  • your maternal grandmother’s clan

For example, when Anzoa was 4 years old, she moved to live with her paternal grandmother from the Padiga clan. She grew up among Padiga people, and 25 years later, still lives in that community.

Although her suru is Ukeyi, the Padiga people consider her part of their kaka — because of the deep bonds formed through shared life, not just ancestry.

Aunties & Uncles

Ma’di has distinct words for aunties and uncles depending on whether they are from the mother’s or father’s side:
  • Mum’s brother - Adrua
  • Mum’s sister - Enderi
  • Dad’s brother - Atari
  • Dad’s sister - A’u

    • ⚠️ Enderi comes from the word for mother which is 'endre' and atari comes from the word for father which is 'ata'
