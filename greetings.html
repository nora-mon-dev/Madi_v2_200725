Greetings (Lazi) 👋🏿

In this section you will learn common greetings and how to introduce yourself.

General Greetings

Lazi is the Ma’di word for "greeting." You’ll often hear:

Ma zi anyi ra" – I have greeted you all

Ingoni, Ingoni ku, Ma cui

The most common way to say “hi” or “hello” in Ma’di is:

  • Ingoni – The direct translation is "How?" (however in greeting it means "Hello")
  • Ingoni ku – The direct translation is "How, no" (however, in greeting it means "I’m good" as a response to ‘Ingoni’)

⚠️ Note: Ingoni ku should only be used as a response to Ingoni. It doesn’t work as a response to Nye ingoni ("How are you?").

Instead, when someone asks:

  • Nye ingoni - How are you?

    • You can reply with:

  • Ma cui - I am good/well

    • Common greeting - translations

    English Ma'di
    Hello Ingoni
    I am good/well

    Are you well? Nye cui?

    Personal introductions - translations

    Pronouns

    • I/Me = Ma
      • e.g.Ma ru ni Anzoa i - My name is Anzoa

    • You = Nye
    • Your = Nya
      • e.g. Nya ru ni adii? - What is your name?
      • e.g. Oti Nyi ingwo? - Where were you born?

        • English Ma'di
        What is your name? Nya ru adii?
        My name is Anzoa Ma ru Anzoa i
        How old are you Nya kinya si?
        I am 15 years old Ma kinya mudri limbi tou
        Where were you born Oti nyi ingwole?
        I was born in Uganda Oti ma Uganda
