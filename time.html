Time (Saa)

In English, time is counted from midnight, for example, 1am is considered the first hour of the day.

However, in Ma'di ti, time is traditionally counted from sunrise.

The word for time in Ma'di ti is saa.

To ask 'What is the time?' you’d say: Saa si?

In South Sudan, Ma'di speakers generally follow the Western time system, where the first hour begins at 1am, after midnight.

But in Uganda, Ma'di time follows a different structure:

The first hour of the day begins at 7am, which aligns with the typical time people start their day. Therefore ‘saa alu’ which translates to ‘first hour’ is 7am.

In this section, we’ll focus on Ugandan Ma'di time.

Ugandan Ma'di time is divided into:

  • Saa: the general term for time between 7am - 6pm.
  • Saa ini dri:: which means time of the night, referring to the evening and nighttime hours i.e. 7pm - 6am.
